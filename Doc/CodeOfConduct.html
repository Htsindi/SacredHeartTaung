LEARNER CODE OF CONDUCT 2009

 

All members of the community of Sacred Heart School place a high value on respect, truth, justice, compassion and reconciliation. They show respect for themselves, all members of the community and for property. Learners shall do the work set out for them and work hard to reach their full potential. They shall take pride in themselves, their school, its environs and their work.

They shall present themselves in proper school uniform. School staff will insist on honest effort, commitment and a high standard of behaviour from learners.

 

Disciplinary measures

 

All learners begin the year with 100 points each. They should strive to maintain and keep these points as high as possible. They lose the points each time they commit an offence. More serious offences carry more points.

Sanctions:

The following will result if learners lose points;

(i)                              20 to 49 points –verbal warning

(ii)                            50 to 79 points- written warning in front of parents.

(iii)                          80 to 100 points –immediate expulsion from school.

 

  • Apart from loosing points learners may be given manual work to do around the school compound.
  • Confiscated cell phones, stereos, jewellery, and other gadgets can only be given back to the parent or guardian

 

Please note that learners will not lose marks for ‘petty issues’ e.g. classroom disturbance refers to a situation where a learner really disrupts a lesson in such a way that learning outcomes are jeopardised.

 

Conclusion:

 

The school’s point system of discipline allows learners the opportunity to exactly know where they in relation to discipline.

They also know the result of the offence they commit. As a result learners should be able to conduct themselves in the right manner. The main purpose is not to catch learners when they do wrong, but to help them conduct themselves in the right manner.
