GENERAL

We aim to mould the character of our children through spiritual guidance and quality education so that they may become useful citizens who will serve the society and the country selflessly.

We provide an educational environment conducive to the holistic development of the learner; this includes the formation of attitudes and values inspired by the spirituality of unity in the Catholic Church.

We admit South African boys and girls who are willing to uphold the morality and values based on Christian principles; who have the ability to meet our internal standards of education; whose families are able to commit themselves to our school fees; who have the aptitude to develop and realise their potential as participating, responsible and compassionate citizens of our country.

We are a fully-fledged Catholic School with Religious Education. We also aim to uplift and empower learners who may be from a disadvantaged background and who have the potential to benefit from our kind of school and who will carry the ethos into their community.

Where vacancies exist we accept boys and girls who are not South African citizens.

ENROLMENT PROCEDURE

Requirement for intake:

  1. Learners need to be of the appropriate age group for the grade to ensure that our holistic developmental stages are in place as these impacts future achievements.

    2. To clarify;

    • Grade R learners will be 5 turning 6
    • Grade 1 learners will be 6 turning 7
    • Grade 2 learners will be 7 turning 8
    • Grade 3 learners will be 8 turning 9 etc
  2. Learners need to have completed the previous stage at a recognised school.
  3. There must be a willingness to participate in Religious Education classes and our school sacred masses and services.
  4. Sacred Heart school reserves the right to contact the learner’s previous school to confirm the learner’s performance, if the learner has left the school in good standing or any other pertinent information at the school.
  5. There needs to be a place available. Places are secured on full payment and allocated in that order in the order of enrolment until the classes are full.
  6. Sacred Heart Private Primary SchoolParents/Guardians need to be able to cope with the fee payments and the extras required in order to participate fully.
  7. Parents/Guardians of learners must agree to abide by Sacred Heart School rules, regulations and ethos.
  8. An agreement must be made by parents and learners that they will abide by the school policies
  9. Learners may be required to do an entrance interview or test
  10. The child’s needs should be such that our facilities are suitable for their holistic development
  11. Children of our staff members are given priority in the enrolment process
  12. In case a learner is leaving the school, a clearance form should be filled before a transfer letter is issued.

INCLUSION POLICY

Our present facilities enable us to include children with mild to moderate educational problems, limited emotional difficulties and slight physical disabilities. Where we are confident that we are able to assist these children through remediation and support systems, to develop suitable enduring coping mechanisms which will allow them to be the best that they can be, we can in all conscience admit them.

In cases where prospective learners require greater assistance than our facilities can offer, and where more than two learning areas require significant therapy or where a facilitator is required for the child to function effectively it is our obligation to recommend a suitable alternative. Therefore, offering informed and experienced guidance for solutions to suit the individual child’s specific needs.

APPLICATION PROCEDURES

  1. In all the phases parents and learners are requested to make an appointment to meet the principal at the school.
  2. Parents/Guardians are free to ask about the following during the enrolment process;
    • The ethos of the school
    • Educational matters
    • Facilities
    • Extra murals
    • Aftercare
    • School fees
    • All queries are answered.
  3. All new learners undergo an assessment during the enrolment process.
  4. The admission policy will be given along with an application form and the present fees structures.
  5. Parents/Guardians fill in the application form and pay the required fee in full i.e. registration fee, uniform fee, stationery fee and the first month of schooling fees. Attached a colour ID/passport photo of the learner on the admission form. This form is then placed in the admission file.
  6. Parents are requested to adhere to the requirements of the application form.

CATHOLIC ETHOS

THE ETHOS OF THE CATHOLIC SCHOOL

To ask, ‘what is the ethos of the catholic school?’ is to try to get at the fundamental purpose of the school, its ultimate meaning and where its value system comes from.

In a world, which ignores the human thirst for God, we are called to share the living water of our faith. In a time when there is little reverence for the image of God in the human person, we are summoned to care for the human life with and ultimate respect.

(From This Moment of Promise, Ontario Conference of Catholic Bishops)

The religious Education Policy document for South Africa Catholic School, Fostering Hope, answers the question in this way:

The Catholic School exists as an expression of the Catholic Church’s mission to share the gospel Jesus.

(Fostering Hope p 5)

What is the Catholic School particularly called to do in sharing the gospel? How do we recognise the authentic Catholic School?

  • The authentic Catholic School is founded on the person of Jesus Christ and is enlivened by gospel values.
  • The authentic Catholic School highlights the relevance of our faith to life, and to contemporary culture and concerns.
  • The authentic Catholic School, embedded within the community of believes; share in the evangelising mission of the Church.
  • The authentic Catholic School is committed to quality education and the development of the whole person.
  • The authentic Catholic School does not exist for the Catholic community alone, but is at the service of all members of the community.1

Catholic Schools are at one and the same time places evangelisation, well-rounded education, enculturation and initiation to the dialogue of life among young people of different religious and social backgrounds.

(African synod #102; The Church in Africa)

It is clear from this that Religious Education will from an integral part of the Catholic School curriculum.

The Catholic school, as an organ within the Body of Christ, is called to make Christ visibly present to the world in its own particular way. It is called to bring the light of Christ to every dimension of its educational enterprise. It does this through its Religious Education by offering, in the Catholic tradition, the Good News of Jesus Christ as a light to illumine every individual search for the meaning of life, and the way to live it. It does so, not in competition with other faith traditions, but in a spirit of dialogue, so that all are affirmed in their own, and enriched by one another’s traditions, recognising that the on Holy Spirit is at the heart of every quest for the Truth.

(All are God’s people, p5)

SCHOOL EXPECTATIONS

‘Love your neighbour as yourself; Love conquers all’

RESPECT FOR PEOPLE

Greet all staff and visitors using ‘Sir’ or ‘Mam’.

Respect each other; learners, teachers, school authority, etc

No teasing, bullying or fighting, no swearing or bad language.

Do not hurt someone else ‘on the inside or the outside’.

Say ‘please’, ‘thank you’, ‘excuse me’ and ‘sorry’.

Play fair.

Don’t laugh if someone makes a mistake.

Listen to one another and to your teacher.

Stand back for adults and show respect to them.

Don’t bring any sharp objects or dangerous material which may be uncomfortable for others.

Chewing gums, unconstructive noisemaking or roaming around in class is forbidden.

Make use of the dustbin, do not drop stuff on the class room floor.

RESPECT FOR SELF

  • Wear your full school uniform (as stipulated by the school) to and from school.
  • Hair must always be clean neat and tidy. No dreadlocks or colour may be used.
  • Long hair must be tied back with hair band.
  • Since the uniform is issued at school no other colours are allowed.
  • Wear white or at least 80% white tekkies during sport time and on sports days.
  • Nail varnish may not be worn.
  • Keep nails short and clean.
  • Beads, bungles, rings, ear rings/knobs (except for a small sleeper/stud for girls) may not be worn.
  • Boys may not wear any form of jewellery.
  • Do all your homework and class work on time.
  • Be clean and nice to be near.
  • Give of your best to sport and extra mural activities.
  • Be sure to bring what you need for school each day – lunch, pencils, pens, books, etc.
  • Take care of your money or give it to your class teacher. A teacher has no responsibility whatsoever for money not given to him/her.
  • Cell phones, Ear phones, Toys or other gadgets may not be brought to school. If found they may be confiscated till the end of the term.
  • Loitering around the campus during class time may not be entertained.
  • Punctuality is encouraged in all aspects; in class, assembly etc.
  • Do not leave the school campus without the permission of the teacher.
  • Use no drugs.
  • Co-operate with the school prefects.

RESPECT FOR PROPERTY AND THINGS THAT BELONG TO OTHERS

  • Keep your class, school clean and tidy – do not be a litter bug!
  • Do not steal or take anything that does not belong to you.
  • Keep all school books clean and tidy. Do not break things.
  • Label everything that belongs to you – clothes, shoes, bag books, etc.
  • ‘Found’ money / other properties to be handed over to the class teacher or to the principal.

CONSEQUENSES FOR BREAKING SCHOOL RULES

  • A warning is issued verbally or in writing by the teacher/the principal.
  • If repeated parent(s)/guardian are informed and the school disciplinary committee gets involved.
  • Parents will meet the principal and the school disciplinary committee on serious and persistent cases.
  • All proceedings are recorded and filed.
  • Lost or damaged books or other properties must be paid for, at their replacement value.
  • The principal and teachers will maintain close communication with the parents/guardians.

The aim of discipline is to correct unacceptable behaviour and not to punish, as far as this is reasonably possible.
